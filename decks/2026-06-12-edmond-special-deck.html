LAYER 2/THE DEAL

Pricing backward from ARV

ARV / after repair value: what the house is worth finished, proven by comps: recent sales of similar homes nearby.
The 70% rule: max price = ARV × ~70% − repairs. In 2026 it's a dial: 75% in hot zones, 65% in slow ones.

/EXAMPLE DEAL

ARV $330k · repairs $50k · at 72% → buy at $190k

Looks like $90k profit. It is not. →

/WHAT IT IS

Short-term loans from specialty lenders, secured by the property. Banks won't touch wrecks; hard money will.

  • Rehab released in draws as work passes inspection

LAYER 5/OKLAHOMA TRAPS 01

Questions you didn't know to ask

/HAIL

Central OK is one of America's most hail-prone regions: higher premiums, constant roof replacement, post-storm roofing scammers. "Newer roof" is a real selling point; a trusted roofer is a key vendor.

/CLAY SOIL

Expansive clay swells and shrinks → foundation movement is common. Stair-step brick cracks and stuck doors = stop. A structural engineer costs a few hundred; a foundation surprise turns +$40k into −$10k.

20:09
LAYER 5/OKLAHOMA TRAPS 02

Questions you didn't know to ask

/VACANT HOUSES

Winterize or keep heat on: a burst pipe in a vacant flip is a five-figure event. Standard homeowner's policies often exclude vacancy → builder's risk + vacant policies.

/LANDLORD + TAXES

OK is landlord-friendly with relatively fast evictions: but verify everything your PM says in year one. Property tax ~0.9% effective, reassessed at YOUR purchase price: run rental math on the post-sale bill.
